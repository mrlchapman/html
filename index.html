Guidance to Support Students in Using AI Appropriately

Use this activity to help students understand when AI can support learning, when it becomes risky or inappropriate, and how to be transparent, safe and academically honest.

1. Four principles for appropriate AI use

Students do not need to avoid AI completely. They do need to use it in a way that supports learning rather than replacing their own thinking.

Be honestSay when and how AI has helped you. Follow the rules set by your teacher, assessor or course team.
Keep thinkingUse AI to support ideas, planning, revision and feedback. Do not let it do the whole task for you.
Check carefullyAI can make mistakes, miss context, invent sources or give biased responses. Always verify important information.
Protect privacyDo not enter personal, confidential or sensitive information about yourself, other students, staff, employers or placement settings.

Facilitator prompt: Ask students, “What is the difference between using AI as a coach and using AI as a ghostwriter?”

3. Sorting activity, green, amber or red?

Sort each AI use into the most appropriate category. Green means usually appropriate, amber means ask first or be cautious, red means not appropriate.

🟢 Green

Usually appropriate learning support.

🟠 Amber

Check with staff, explain your use, or use extra caution.

🔴 Red

Likely inappropriate, unsafe or academically dishonest.

Move the cards into the three boxes, then check your answers.

5. Scenario decisions

Read each scenario and choose the best response.

Scenario 1

Essay plan

A student asks AI to suggest an essay structure, then writes the essay themselves and checks the points against class notes.

Scenario 2

Personal data

A student copies a real safeguarding concern, including names and personal details, into an AI chatbot to get advice.

Scenario 3

Full assignment

A student asks AI to complete the whole assignment, then submits it as their own work.

Choose one response for each scenario, then check your answers.
